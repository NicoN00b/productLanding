About Me

I have worked in the library sciences field for most of my adult life, which has given me ample opportunity to work with various software platforms and hardware. I spend a lot of time troubeshooting and training people with various levels of skill. At home, I have explored various Linux Distros (Fedora was a challenging and rewarding experiment for me) and work with both Mac OS and Windows. My academic background is completely devoid of computer sciences, but heavy on Comparative Literature, Critical Theory, and Philosophy (mostly useless in an economic sense).

Quite a few friends have left other careers to pursue futures in programming or other tech jobs. I am still exploring what I want to do, but technical writing and user experience are both interesting fields. I am also very interested in cybersecurity.

In the rest of my life, I enjoy biking, playing basketball, hiking, mushroom hunting, pinball, playing music and writing, and watching bad movies on vhs. I also enjoy oxford commas and reading.
